Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before visiting beautiful Taniti.

💵 What currency is used on Taniti?

Taniti uses the U.S. Dollar (USD). Many businesses also accept major credit cards, euros, and Japanese yen.

🔌 What type of electrical outlets are available?

Power outlets are 120 volts, the same standard used in the United States.

🗣 What language is spoken?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. In rural areas, English may be less common among older residents.

🏥 Is medical care available?

Yes. Taniti has one hospital and several medical clinics. The hospital has multilingual employees to assist visitors.

🚔 Is Taniti safe?

Taniti is considered a very safe destination. Violent crime is rare, but visitors should remain aware of occasional pickpocketing in busy tourist areas.

🍺 What is the drinking age?

The legal drinking age is 18. Alcohol is not sold or served between midnight and 9:00 a.m.

🚌 How do I get around the island?

Visitors can travel using public buses, taxis, rental cars, bicycles, or by walking through Taniti City and Merriton Landing.

✈ How do visitors arrive?

Most visitors arrive by airplane. A small cruise ship also docks at Yellow Leaf Bay once each week.

🎉 Are attractions open every day?

Taniti celebrates many national holidays. Some attractions and restaurants may close during holidays, so visitors should plan ahead.

Helpful Travel Tips

  • ✔ Bring comfortable walking shoes.
  • ✔ Book lodging early during peak travel seasons.
  • ✔ Carry sunscreen and stay hydrated.
  • ✔ Keep valuables secure while sightseeing.
  • ✔ Respect local customs and island wildlife.
  • ✔ Explore Merriton Landing for shopping, dining, and entertainment.
